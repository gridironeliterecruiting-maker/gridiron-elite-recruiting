## TL;DR

For a $100K raise at this stage (MVP built, 2 test users, pre-meaningful-revenue), Paul should use a **SAFE note with a $500K–$1M valuation cap**. This is the simplest, cheapest, and most founder-friendly structure. Don't do a priced round — it's overkill and expensive for $100K.

## 2. Recommended Deal Structure: SAFE Note

### Why SAFE (not convertible note, not priced round)

| Structure | Recommendation | Why |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| **SAFE note** | ✅ **USE THIS** | No legal fees ($0–$500 with YC template), no interest, no maturity date, simple |
| Convertible note | ❌ Avoid | Has interest (6–8%), maturity date (creates pressure), more complex |
| Priced round | ❌ Avoid | Legal costs $5K–$15K, requires board setup, overkill for $100K |

**Use the YC Post-Money SAFE** — it's the industry standard, free template, and investors know it. Download from [ycombinator.com/documents](https://www.ycombinator.com/documents).

## 4. What Paul Should NOT Give Away

### Hard No's 🚫
1. **More than 25% equity** — anything above this for $100K undervalues the company and makes future fundraising painful
2. **Board control** — Paul should always control the board at this stage. If giving a board seat, keep it 2-1 (founder majority)
3. **Full ratchet anti-dilution** — this would crush Paul if he ever raises a down round. Accept "broad-based weighted average" only
4. **Liquidation preference above 1x** — investor gets their $100K back first in a sale, that's fair. 2x or 3x is not
5. **Vesting on Paul's existing shares** — Paul built this. His shares should be fully vested or on a very founder-friendly schedule. DO NOT accept 4-year vesting on 100% of founder shares for a $100K investment
6. **Exclusivity / non-compete on Paul personally** — don't let an investor lock Paul into the company if things go sideways
7. **Approval rights on spending** — investor shouldn't need to approve individual expenses. Quarterly reporting is enough transparency

### Be Careful With ⚠️
- **Pro-rata rights** are standard and fine — let the investor maintain their percentage in future rounds
- **Information rights** are fine — quarterly updates are standard
- **Right of first refusal** on future rounds — acceptable but not ideal

## 5. Industry & Market Context

### Sports Recruiting Tech Landscape
- **NCSA (Next College Student Athlete)** — acquired by IMG Academy, charges families $3,000–$8,000+. Market leader.
- **Hudl** — valued at $4B+ (2024). Primarily video but expanding into recruiting.
- **FieldLevel** — free for athletes, monetizes via college coach subscriptions
- **SportsRecruits** — similar model, mid-market
- **On3 / 247Sports** — media + rankings, less direct recruiting tools

### Paul's Competitive Advantage
- **Price disruption** — NCSA charges $3K–$8K. If Paul charges $50–$200/mo ($600–$2,400/yr), he's significantly cheaper
- **Direct outreach** — Instantly.ai email campaigns to coaches is a differentiator most platforms don't offer
- **Football-specific** — focused niche vs. generalist platforms
- **Built-in pipeline** — Iowa Gridiron Elite youth program is a natural customer funnel

### Market Size (rough estimates)
- ~1.1 million high school football players in the US annually
- ~300,000 actively seeking college recruitment
- If Paul captures just 1% at $100/mo = $3.6M ARR potential
- TAM for college sports recruiting software market is growing, estimated multi-billion globally across all sports

### Valuation Comparables
At pre-seed with an MVP and minimal traction:
- Typical pre-seed valuations: $500K–$2M for solo founders with working product
- Sports tech specifically tends toward the lower end unless there's viral traction
- $500K–$1M post-money valuation cap is **realistic and defensible** for Gridiron Elite's current stage

## 7. Paul's Negotiation Playbook

### Before the Meeting
1. **Know your BATNA** — can Paul bootstrap this without the $100K? If yes, his negotiating position is stronger
2. **Have a one-pager** ready: product screenshot, market size, use of funds, ask
3. **Practice the pitch**: "I built a working recruiting platform, have 2 athletes testing it, and come from running Iowa Gridiron Elite. $100K gets me to 50 paying customers in 12 months."

### In the Meeting
1. **Open with Option B** ($650K cap, ~15% equity)
2. **Anchor high** — mention that pre-seed caps in sports tech range $500K–$2M
3. **Emphasize capital efficiency** — $65/mo operating costs is insanely lean
4. **Highlight the moat** — coach database + direct email outreach + built-in athlete pipeline from youth program
5. **If they push for more equity**, move to 20% max, not beyond

### Red Flags from Investors 🚩
- Asking for 40%+ equity → they're not a real startup investor
- Wanting to be CEO or co-founder → walk away
- "I want my money back in 2 years guaranteed" → that's a loan, not an investment
- Requiring personal guarantee → absolutely not
